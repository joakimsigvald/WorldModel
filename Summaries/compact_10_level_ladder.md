# Compact 10-Level Ontological Ladder  
### With Approximate Compression Factors Between Levels

This document presents the compact 10-level hierarchy along with estimated compression factors:  
how many level-k subjects participate in forming one subject at level (k+1).

---

## **Level-by-Level Summary**

### **Level 1 → Level 2**  
**Bit → Pair**  
**Compression:** ×2  
Two bits bind into one pair.

---

### **Level 2 → Level 3**  
**Pair → Loop**  
**Compression:** ×3–4  
Three or four pairs form a loop.

---

### **Level 3 → Level 4**  
**Loop → Particle**  
**Compression:** ×10  
A handful of coordinated loops generate a particle excitation.

---

### **Level 4 → Level 5**  
**Particle → Atom**  
**Compression:** ×20  
Electrons and nucleons (≈20 particles for carbon) form an atom.

---

### **Level 5 → Level 6**  
**Atom → Protein / Complex Molecule**  
**Compression:** ×10³  
Biomolecules typically incorporate thousands of atoms.

---

### **Level 6 → Level 7**  
**Protein → Organelle**  
**Compression:** ×10³  
Organelles contain thousands of proteins.

---

### **Level 7 → Level 8**  
**Organelle → Cell**  
**Compression:** ×10²–10³  
Cells contain hundreds to thousands of organelles.

---

### **Level 8 → Level 9**  
**Cell → Microcircuit**  
**Compression:** ×10²–10³  
Minimal neural microcircuits involve hundreds to thousands of neurons.

---

### **Level 9 → Level 10**  
**Microcircuit → Mind**  
**Compression:** ×10³–10⁴  
Thousands to tens of thousands of microcircuits integrate into a unified conscious subject.
