## 2. Why Not Neutrinos?
We initially considered neutrinos as first mapping candidate but rejected them because:

### 2.1 Problems Identified
- **Extremely small mass** → too hard to explain without sophisticated mechanisms.  
- **Three neutrino flavours** → introduces unnecessary complexity too early.  
- **Left-handed only** → requires building chirality & weak-interaction structure before mass/charge.  
- **Mixing/oscillation** → advanced phenomena, not ideal for first-mapping.

### Conclusion
**Neutrinos are too complicated** to model first and require structures we haven’t built yet.

## 7. Merged Picture: Icosahedral Electron Core
### 7.1 Electron
- A **cluster of 3-loops** arranged in a roughly **icosahedral core**, inside the spacetime grid.  
- The core **pushes outward** on the surrounding space → negative charge.  
- Distorted 3-loop environment → mass and inertia.

### 7.2 Positron
- Same icosahedral defect but slightly **contracted**, pulling space inward.  
- Pull = positive charge.

### 7.3 Photon connection
- Photon = **icosahedral 4-loop shell** in the EM layer.  
- Electron/positron = **icosahedral 3-loop core** inside spacetime.

Thus,  
- **Photon** = hollow shell  
- **Electron** = filled core  
- **Positron** = inverted core  

Three different uses of the same symmetry.

### 7.4 Annihilation
Opposite distortions cancel → spacetime relaxes → excess energy becomes dissolved 4-loops → photon icosahedra.

This matches electron–positron annihilation perfectly.
